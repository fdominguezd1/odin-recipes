Aborrajado, Valle del Cauca special dish

Aborrajado, the Valle del Cauca special plantain, flour, cheese and guava sweet dish

Aborrajado is a typical Valluno dish, which uses very ripe plantains and cheese. To prepare it, thin slices of plantain are removed, fried and filled with cheese and bocadillo (guava sweet). Then, it is covered with a mixture of flour, milk and eggs; finally, sugar and salt are added.

Ingredients

  • 4 Spoons of wheat flour
  • 2 Eggs
  • 250 gr of Colombian campesino cheese. If you don't have it, you can use any other simple melting fresh cheese
  • Salt and vegetable oil
  • 2 Spoons of milk
  • 1 Spoon of sugar
  • 2 Plantains (it has to be ripped plantain)

Preparation

  1. Step 1

    2. Peel and cut the plantains into small pieces.

  2. Step 2

    3. Fry in hot oil until lightly browned. Remove and drain.

  3. Step 3

    4. Then put the cheese in half of two Patacones (fried plantains), press the edges.

  4. Step 4

    5. Beat the eggs, add the milk and mix well.

  5. Step 5

    6. Mash the plantains to a thin layer

  6. Step 6

    7. Add the flour and sugar until a creamy consistency.

  7. Step 7

    8. Soak the plantains well with the cheese, and fry in hot oil until golden brown. Drain and serve hot.
